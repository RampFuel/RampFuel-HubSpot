Is a migration recommended?

Yes, but a light one. Renames are label-only and move no data. The only real data migration is the small number of deals sitting in stages being retired. The three empty pipelines carry zero risk.

StepAction
1Export all 51 deals with their current pipeline and stage as a backup baseline
2Archive the 3 empty pipelines (Partnership, Investor, Shared Selling). No deals, no migration
3Rename labels in place (Lead becomes New, etc.). Deals keep their stage, nothing moves
4Remap the 5 deals in "Assessment" and the 1 in "Active Client" to their correct sell or delivery stage, then delete those stages
5Convert "On Hold" and "Kicked Off" handling: add an On Hold flag property, remove the stages
6Update any workflows, reports, or saved views that reference the old stage IDs
7Do it in a low-traffic window and tell the two active reps before the stage names change
Bottom line: the migration is small. With only 51 deals and roughly 6 sitting in stages you are retiring, this is a one-sitting job, not a project. The empty-pipeline cleanup is zero-risk and can happen immediately.

Why Change

Six structural recommendations, ranked by impact. Click any item for the reason and the live evidence.

1 Separate relationship type from lifecycle stage Highest impact ›
WhyLifecycle stage is the funnel. A partner is a relationship, not a funnel position. Encoding "Partner" as a lifecycle stage inflates conversion and blocks suppression and attribution.
EvidenceCustom "Partner" lifecycle stage (id 1661347552) holds 3 contacts; 21 more partners/internal sit in "Other"; ~70 across the base.
FixAdd a Relationship Type property on contacts and companies, domain-driven, off the funnel axis.
2 Add a qualification gate to the funnel High ›
WhyToday leads jump straight to Opportunity with no defined gate, so the 5% conversion is meaningless and reps cannot prioritize.
EvidenceSubscriber, MQL, and SQL stages are all empty (0). Funnel is just Lead, Opportunity, Customer.
FixDefine New, Qualified (SQL), Opportunity, Customer with entry criteria for each step.
3 Retire the 3 empty pipelines and collapse stage sprawl High ›
WhyEmpty pipelines and duplicate stage names make cross-pipeline reporting and win-rate math impossible.
Evidence5 pipelines, all 51 deals in 2 of them; 45 stages with "Closed Won" and "Closed Lost" each defined 5 times.
FixKeep Sales and TopDown; archive the rest; standardize one stage convention.
4 Track partners as a lead source High ›
WhyPartners generate referred business but get no credit, so you cannot see or justify the value of Pax8, SherWeb, Ingram, or TD Synnex.
EvidenceHeavy partner presence, zero Lead Source or sourcing-partner fields on contacts or deals.
FixAdd Lead Source and Sourcing Partner; credit referrals while keeping partner contacts suppressed.
5 Clean up deactivated owners Medium ›
WhyRecords on deactivated users drop out of views, break routing, and skew per-rep numbers.
Evidence8 owners, only 5 active; Arya Masaeli (inactive) still owns a deal.
FixReassign, remove inactive owners, add a routing rule.
6 Stop using "Other" as a catch-all Medium ›
Why"Other" hides what records are, which is why partners are invisible in the funnel today.
Evidence21 contacts in "Other", mostly pax8.com and internal.
FixReplace "Other" with the Relationship Type property.

Change List to the Sweet Spot

The fifteen changes that move you from the current flow to the target flow, grouped by area.

Structure & Funnel

  1. Remove Partner from lifecycle; add Relationship Type property
  2. Define a gated funnel: New, Qualified (SQL), Opportunity, Customer
  3. Retire the 3 empty deal pipelines
  4. Standardize deal stages; remove duplicate labels
  5. Reassign records off inactive owners; add routing rule

Data

  1. Triage the Aug 2025 import (326 unassigned): validate, suppress cold, assign real leads
  2. Delete ~45 blank company shells and bot/spam contacts
  3. Merge cross-email duplicate people
  4. Fix $1 and blank deal amounts
  5. Add field validation so subjects/notes stop landing in name fields

Partner Sourcing

  1. Add a Lead Source property (value: Partner Referral)
  2. Add a Sourcing Partner property / association to the partner company
  3. Tag partner-referred contacts and deals
  4. Build a partner-sourced pipeline and revenue report
  5. Suppress partners from prospecting, keep them credited as sources
Sweet spot: records are classified at entry, the funnel holds only real prospects through defined gates, partners are suppressed from outbound yet credited as lead sources, and one maintained domain list drives every classification.
